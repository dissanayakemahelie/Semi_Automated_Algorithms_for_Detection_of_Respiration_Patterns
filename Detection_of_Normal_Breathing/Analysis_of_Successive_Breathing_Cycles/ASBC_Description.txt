Analysis of Successive Breathing Cycles assesses periodicity in non-stationary time series by sliding a window of fixed length to evaluate the similarity between the signal and the delayed versions across the time series.
The core idea is that if a signal exhibits periodic behaviour, then segments of the signal separated by the system's natural period will show high similarity. A delay-based self-similarity measure is computed using the windowed sum of squared differences.
