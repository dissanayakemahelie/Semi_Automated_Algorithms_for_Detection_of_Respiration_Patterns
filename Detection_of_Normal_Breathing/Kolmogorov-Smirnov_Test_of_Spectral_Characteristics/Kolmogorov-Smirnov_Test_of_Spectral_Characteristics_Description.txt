In order to distinguish normal breathing patterns from non-normal breathing patterns based on their frequency content, a spectral entropy analysis is performed using time-frequency methods.
The spectral properties of physically periodic signals differ significantly from non-periodic signals, as characterised by the spectral entropy.
To extract the necessary frequency information, the power spectral density is calculated from the instantaneous amplitudeof the signal, employing non-overlapping sliding windows of two different lengths: 10,000 samples and 12,000 samples. This is implemented with both the Short-Time Fourier Transformation (STFT) and Thomson's Multitaper Method (TMM).
Results are shown in Figure Kolmogorov-Smirnov_Test_of_Spectral_Characteristics_STFT_Results.pdf and Kolmogorov-Smirnov_Test_of_Spectral_Characteristics_TMM_Results.pdf. Green as normal breathing and blue as non-normal breathing patterns.
