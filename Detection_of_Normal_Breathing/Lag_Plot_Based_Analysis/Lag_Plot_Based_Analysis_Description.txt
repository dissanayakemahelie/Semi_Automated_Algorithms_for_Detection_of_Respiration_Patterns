To characterise the temporal dynamics of breathing signals, a circularity analysis based on lag plots is performed on the positive peaks of the time-dependent displacement signal for all datasets, with as the reference dataset. This method captures and compares the cycle-to-cycle variability in breathing patterns. Random time series data produce lag plots with evenly scattered points, whereas periodic structures, such as normal breathing, exhibit circular or sinusoidal lag plot patterns. This geometric regularity is exploited in this method.
